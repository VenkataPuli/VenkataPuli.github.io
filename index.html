Markets that solve the last-mile logistics challenge in grocery will define the next decade of e-commerce growth.

⚡ Impact Summary

Proactive seller education — deployed before escalation, not after — was 2.9× more effective than reactive case resolution at reducing repeat violations. The 47 sellers contacted proactively generated 52% fewer repeat cases over the following 8 weeks, versus 18% reduction in the non-contacted group. This is the core insight: Account Health value is created upstream of the violation, not in processing it.

What I Learned — Data as a Prevention Tool

"The 35% reduction didn't come from working harder on individual cases. It came from stepping back and asking: why are the same sellers violating the same policies repeatedly? The Case Management System data made the answer obvious — Health & Beauty and Electronics sellers were generating the bulk of violations because they were entering high-complexity regulatory environments without understanding what was required of them. Once I identified that pattern and started reaching out proactively — before a violation became a suspension — the recurrence rate dropped sharply. The lesson is that data is most valuable when it tells you where to intervene before something breaks, not just when it confirms that something already has."
